🎯 Welcome to JobSift

This tool searches Jobright.ai and direct company careers pages, filters roles against your criteria, and scores what’s left so you can focus on the best matches.

This quick setup takes about 5 minutes. Every setting can be refined anytime in the full config editor.

Before you begin:
1. A Chromium-based browser is required. The Jobright scraper automates a browser session to collect results. Microsoft Edge (pre-installed on Windows) works out of the box. Google Chrome also works.
2. A Jobright.ai account with a completed profile. JobSift reads match scores that Jobright generates from your profile. Make sure your Jobright profile is filled out. Uploading your resume is the quickest way to populate it.

🔍 What roles are you looking for?

These title terms become the first filter for your first track. The filter checks only the job title: a posting must match at least one required term to qualify; any title matching a blocked term is dropped immediately.

Title-only filtering is broad. Adding “Engineer” will match software, civil, and chemical engineers equally. The next two steps address that with industry fit scoring and description-level blockers. For now, add the title terms that describe your target roles.

Type a term and press Enter to add it.

🎯 Your First Track

Think of a track as a saved search with a scoring bar attached. It defines the work arrangement and the minimum salary that makes a role worth your time. The title terms you set in the previous step are already assigned to this track.

Example: a Copywriter might set up two tracks: Remote ($90K+) for fully remote roles anywhere in the US, and Los Angeles Hybrid ($110K+) for local roles worth commuting to. Both tracks evaluate the same postings; a role passes if it clears either bar.

Set up your first track below. You can duplicate and tweak it in the full editor to add more.

🎓 Industry Fit

Title filters catch the right roles; domain scoring catches the right companies. A scraper searching for “Engineer” returns software engineers, civil engineers, aerospace contractors, and chemical plant roles with identical title matches.

Domain scoring adds a bonus when a company or posting signals it operates in your target industry. Tier 5 is ideal fit (e.g. a named cybersecurity vendor); Tier 4 is strong fit (e.g. “cybersecurity”, “infosec”, “zero trust”). These keywords are matched against the company name and job description.

Leave both lists empty if your industry is unambiguous. If your title is shared across sectors, add terms here to surface the right ones.

You can tune scoring thresholds and add Tier 3/2 bands in the full editor after setup.

🎉 You’re Set Up

Nice work. Here’s what you just configured:
  • Title filters (required and blocked terms)
  • Your first search track (work type, salary floor, location)
  • Industry fit scoring (Tier 5 and Tier 4 domain signals)
  • Jobright search source and optional direct career pages
  • Hard-reject blockers (industries, companies, description keywords)
What’s still worth configuring in the full editor:
  • Scoring thresholds: Grade A/B/C cutoffs and salary score tiers are set to defaults. Tune them in the Tracks tab to match your market.
  • Additional tracks: open the Tracks tab, click Duplicate on your first track, then adjust the copy for a different work arrangement or salary bar.
  • More ATS boards: add Greenhouse, Lever, or Ashby slugs for target companies in the Sources tab.
  • Routing rules: in Sources under Advanced Routing, you can assign specific tracks to specific Jobright searches for fine-grained control.
  • Scoring tiers D2/D3: mid-tier domain signals and seniority scoring for finer-grained ranking, in the Scoring tab.
